Gap Year

I was 19 years old the first time I went to India. I stayed there during the summer volunterring in a slum school, learning more than I was teaching. Until today I don't know exactly what guided me there in the first place, but I was sure I was grasping something I needed for myself. I knew I had encountered a different place where I felt alive, perplex and intrigued in every single moment. I wanted to come back to that astonishment feeling, and there I went for six months, between my bachelor and master degree. During this complex and intense time I volunteer, I learnt, I traveled and brought with me lots of people, places but mainly the ability to love, compreheend and share with the other, as they do it so naturally in India. It was an experience that marked me and importantly something made in the right moment.
